Lead Disclosure Requirements

Under 42 U.S.C. § 4852d and 40 CFR Part 745, sellers and landlords of pre-1978 residential housing must disclose all known lead-based paint hazards before sale or lease.

  • Provide the EPA pamphlet "Protect Your Family from Lead in Your Home"
  • Complete and retain a signed Lead Disclosure Addendum for at least 3 years
  • Buyers receive a 10-day inspection period before purchase obligation
  • Applies to most residential properties with 4 or fewer units built before 1978
  • Penalties up to $19,507 per violation for failure to disclose

Exemptions include housing built in 1978 or later, zero-bedroom units, and housing for the elderly or disabled (unless a child under 6 resides or is expected to reside).

Cleveland Lead Requirements

Cleveland's Lead Safe Housing Ordinance (Chapter 365) requires landlords to obtain a Lead Safe Certificate before renting any pre-1978 residential property.

  • Certificate valid for 2 years; renewal requires re-inspection
  • Visual assessment plus dust-wipe sampling required to pass clearance
  • Properties where a child under 6 resides trigger additional clearance testing
  • Certificates managed by the Cleveland Department of Public Health, Division of Environment
  • Non-compliant landlords may face rental registration suspension and fines

Cleveland is one of the strictest municipalities in Ohio — properties must achieve clearance levels below EPA standards. LeadSafe Ohio assessments are designed to meet and document this threshold.

Ohio Approved Encapsulants

Ohio EPA maintains a registry of approved encapsulants under Ohio Administrative Code 3701-32. Encapsulation seals lead in place with a durable bonded coating.

  • Products must meet ASTM E1795 (non-membrane) or ASTM E1797 (membrane-type) standards
  • Commonly approved products include Fiberlock Safe Encapsulant, SealBest, and Zinsser Bulls Eye 1-2-3
  • Application must be performed by an Ohio-certified lead abatement contractor
  • Re-inspection typically required every 1–3 years depending on surface condition
  • Encapsulation does not qualify as full abatement — underlying hazard must be documented

LeadSafe Ohio can identify whether encapsulation is appropriate and provide documentation suitable for Ohio EPA and HUD compliance records.

Data Backups & Best Practices

Data loss is not a matter of if — it's when. Hardware fails, ransomware encrypts, and humans delete things. A proper backup strategy means the difference between a bad afternoon and a business-ending event.

  • The 3-2-1 Rule: Keep 3 copies of your data, on 2 different media types, with 1 stored offsite or in the cloud
  • Backup frequency: Daily incremental backups; full backups weekly — match your schedule to how much data you can afford to lose
  • Test your restores: A backup you've never tested is not a backup — schedule quarterly restore drills
  • Ransomware protection: Use air-gapped or immutable backups that cannot be overwritten by an attacker
  • Encryption: All backup data — local and cloud — should be encrypted at rest and in transit
  • Retention policy: Keep at least 30 days of restore points; compliance industries often require 7 years
  • RTO vs. RPO: Define your Recovery Time Objective and Recovery Point Objective up front

GuardPoint Technology can assess your backup posture and implement automated, monitored solutions using Veeam, Acronis, Backblaze B2, or cloud-native options — sized for small business.

On-Site vs. Hosted Solutions & Cost Savings

Choosing between on-premise hardware and cloud-hosted services is one of the most impactful technology decisions a small business makes — and the right answer depends on your size, data sensitivity, and budget.

  • On-site: Higher upfront hardware cost, full data control, no recurring fees — but requires local maintenance, power, cooling, and hardware refresh every 3–5 years
  • Hosted / cloud: Predictable monthly costs, scales easily, vendor manages updates — ideal for small teams and remote workers
  • Hybrid approach: Keep sensitive data on-prem; use cloud for email, collaboration, and disaster recovery — the best of both worlds for most SMBs
  • Hidden on-prem costs: Don't forget electricity, rack space, IT labor, and the cost of downtime when hardware fails
  • Real savings example: Moving from in-house Exchange to Microsoft 365 typically saves $3,000–$8,000/year when TCO is calculated over 5 years
  • When on-prem still wins: Data sovereignty requirements, heavy compliance environments, or high-bandwidth local storage needs

GuardPoint Technology provides a free Total Cost of Ownership analysis to help you make a data-driven decision — not a vendor-driven one.
